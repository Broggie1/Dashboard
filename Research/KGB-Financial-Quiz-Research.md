## Quiz Best Practices

### 1. Question Sequencing
**Principle:** Easy → Detailed (Progressive Disclosure)

**Why it works:**
- Reduces friction (start with non-threatening questions)
- Builds commitment (invested time = higher completion)
- Personalizes experience (skip irrelevant questions)

**Example Flow:**
1. Demographics (age, family) - EASY
2. Financial situation (income, homeowner) - MODERATE
3. Current coverage (life insurance, pension) - DETAILED
4. Gaps & urgency (worries, timeline) - PERSONALIZED

### 2. Engagement Tactics
**Progress Bar:** Show % completion (increases finish rate by 20-30%)  
**Visual Feedback:** Checkmarks, animations (gamification)  
**Personalization:** Use name, reference previous answers  
**Urgency:** "95% of families are underinsured" (social proof)

### 3. Email Capture Timing
**Best practice:** Capture BEFORE results (not at start)

**Why:** Users want their personalized report, so they'll give email to get it.

**Flow:**
- Questions 1-19: No email required
- Question 20: "Enter email to get your personalized Financial Protection Report"
- Results page: Immediately show report (build trust)

### 4. Results Presentation
**Format:** Score + Gap Analysis + Recommendations

**Example:**
> **Your Financial Protection Score: 62/100**
> 
> ✅ **Life Insurance:** Well-covered (€300k term life)  
> ⚠️ **Health Insurance:** Basic plan, consider upgrade  
> ❌ **Income Protection:** No coverage (high risk)  
> ✅ **Pension:** On track (8% contribution)
> 
> **Top 3 Priorities:**
> 1. Get income protection quote (covers 75% salary if unable to work)
> 2. Review health insurance plan (compare family coverage options)
> 3. Optimize pension contributions (increase to 10% for tax relief)
>
> **Next Step:** Book free 30-min consultation with KGB advisor

## Financial FAQ Database (200+ Q&As)

### Life Insurance

**Q: What is term life insurance vs whole life insurance?**  
A: Term life insurance covers you for a fixed period (e.g., 20 years) and is cheaper. Whole life insurance covers you for your entire life and builds cash value, but costs more. Most families choose term life for affordability.

**Q: How much life insurance coverage do I need?**  
A: A common rule is 10x your annual salary, or enough to cover debts + 5-7 years of income replacement. Example: €50k salary = €500k coverage. Adjust based on dependents and mortgage.

**Q: Do I need life insurance if I'm single with no kids?**  
A: If you have debts (student loans, credit cards) or aging parents who depend on you financially, yes. Otherwise, it's optional but can lock in low rates while you're young and healthy.

**Q: What factors affect life insurance premiums?**  
A: Age (younger = cheaper), health (medical exam required), smoking status (non-smokers pay 50% less), coverage amount, and term length. Ireland-specific: occupation and hobbies (e.g., dangerous jobs cost more).

**Q: What is mortgage protection insurance?**  
A: Required in Ireland if you have a mortgage. It pays off your mortgage if you die before it's repaid. Usually decreasing term insurance (coverage reduces as mortgage balance decreases).

**Q: Can I get life insurance with pre-existing conditions?**  
A: Yes, but premiums may be higher, or certain conditions excluded. Disclose all conditions upfront to avoid claim denial. Some insurers specialize in high-risk cases.

**Q: What is decreasing term insurance?**  
A: Coverage amount reduces over time (e.g., from €300k to €0 over 20 years). Used for mortgage protection (as your debt decreases, coverage decreases). Cheaper than level term insurance.

**Q: Is there tax relief on life insurance in Ireland?**  
A: No direct tax relief on life insurance premiums, but life insurance payouts are generally tax-free for beneficiaries. Pension-linked life insurance may have different tax treatment.

### Health Insurance

**Q: What's the difference between private and public healthcare in Ireland?**  
A: Public (HSE) is free but can have long wait times for non-emergency care. Private health insurance gives faster access to specialists, private hospitals, and more choice of consultants.

**Q: What does health insurance cover?**  
A: Varies by plan. Typically: hospital stays, surgery, consultant fees, diagnostic tests. May exclude: GP visits, dental, optical, pre-existing conditions (waiting periods apply).

**Q: Should I get a family plan or individual plans?**  
A: Family plans cover spouse + kids under one policy, often cheaper than multiple individual plans. Consider if children need orthodontics or have health issues (family plan usually better value).

**Q: What are pre-existing condition waiting periods?**  
A: New health insurance policies in Ireland have waiting periods (typically 5 years) for pre-existing conditions. Example: if you have diabetes, diabetes-related claims may not be covered for 5 years.

**Q: Is there tax relief on health insurance in Ireland?**  
A: Yes! Tax relief at source (TRS) reduces your premium by 20% automatically. Example: €1,000 premium = €800 after tax relief. Applied by insurer, not on tax return.

**Q: When should I upgrade my health insurance plan?**  
A: When you need faster access (long public wait times), having kids (maternity/pediatrics), aging (more health issues), or if current plan has high out-of-pocket costs.

**Q: What is excess and how does it work?**  
A: Excess is the amount you pay before insurance kicks in. Example: €100 excess = you pay first €100 of each claim. Higher excess = lower premium. Choose based on how often you use insurance.

### Mortgages

**Q: What are first-time buyer requirements in Ireland?**  
A: Must be buying first home, income within limits (€50k single, €75k joint for Help to Buy), pass mortgage stress test, have deposit (usually 10% for first-time buyers, 20% for others).

**Q: How much deposit do I need?**  
A: First-time buyers: 10% (Central Bank rule). Second/subsequent buyers: 20%. Example: €300k home = €30k deposit (first-time) or €60k (others). Some exceptions for affordable housing schemes.

**Q: Fixed vs variable mortgage rates - which is better?**  
A: Fixed rate: Same rate for 2-5 years (predictable payments, protected from rate increases). Variable: Can change anytime (lower initially, risk of increases). Choose fixed if rates are low and you want certainty.

**Q: How much can I borrow?**  
A: Generally 3.5x gross salary (joint incomes combined). Example: €50k salary = €175k mortgage. Central Bank may allow 4x for some first-time buyers. Stress-tested at higher rates to ensure affordability.

**Q: What is APR vs interest rate?**  
A: Interest rate is the cost to borrow (e.g., 3.5%). APR (Annual Percentage Rate) includes interest + fees (e.g., 3.8%), giving true cost. Always compare APRs, not just interest rates.

**Q: Is mortgage protection insurance required in Ireland?**  
A: Yes, lenders require it. Pays off mortgage if you die before it's repaid. Usually decreasing term life insurance. Shop around (don't accept lender's default offer, can be 20-30% more expensive).

**Q: What is the Help to Buy scheme?**  
A: Irish government scheme giving first-time buyers up to €30,000 tax rebate toward deposit for new builds. Conditions: first-time buyer, income <€50k (single) or €75k (joint), property <€500k.

**Q: Should I switch my mortgage?**  
A: If you can save 0.5%+ on rate and have 12+ years remaining, yes. Example: €200k mortgage at 4% → 3% saves €2,000/year. Watch for switching fees, legal costs, break fees (if fixed rate).

### Pensions

**Q: What is a defined benefit vs defined contribution pension?**  
A: Defined benefit: Guaranteed income in retirement (e.g., 50% of final salary). Rare now. Defined contribution: You + employer contribute, final amount depends on investment performance. Most Irish pensions are defined contribution.

**Q: What is a PRSA (Personal Retirement Savings Account)?**  
A: Irish pension for self-employed or those without employer pensions. Tax-deductible contributions, grows tax-free, access at 60+. Portable (keep if you change jobs). Max annual contribution: age-dependent.

**Q: Is there tax relief on pension contributions in Ireland?**  
A: Yes! Up to age-dependent limits (e.g., 25% of salary under 30, 40% over 60). Example: €50k salary, age 35 → max €10k contribution with tax relief at your marginal rate (20% or 40%).

**Q: When can I access my pension?**  
A: Age 60 minimum (PRSAs), 65 for some schemes. Early access penalties apply. At retirement, take 25% tax-free lump sum, rest as taxable income or annuity.

**Q: How much should I contribute to my pension?**  
A: Rule of thumb: Half your age as a % when you start. Example: start at 30 = 15% of salary. Earlier you start, less needed per year. Compound growth makes early contributions worth 2-3x later ones.

**Q: What if my employer doesn't match pension contributions?**  
A: You're missing free money. Negotiate employer match (even 3-5% helps). If refused, contribute to PRSA yourself to get tax relief. Switching jobs for better benefits may be worth it long-term.

**Q: What pension options for self-employed?**  
A: PRSA (most flexible), Executive Pension (if you own limited company, higher contribution limits). Both offer tax relief. Start early (compound growth) and review annually to maximize contributions.

### Income Protection

**Q: What is income protection insurance?**  
A: Replaces 75% of your salary if you can't work due to illness/injury (not unemployment). Pays until you return to work, retire, or policy ends. Example: €50k salary = €37.5k/year income protection.

**Q: How much of my salary is covered?**  
A: Typically 75% of gross salary (some policies 50-80%). Lower than 100% to incentivize return to work. Tax-free payout means take-home is similar to working income.

**Q: What are waiting periods (deferred period)?**  
A: Time before payments start (typically 13, 26, or 52 weeks). Longer waiting period = lower premium. Choose based on sick pay from employer and emergency savings. Example: 6 months employer sick pay = choose 26-week deferred period.

**Q: How is income protection taxed in Ireland?**  
A: Premiums paid by employer: Taxable benefit. Payout: Tax-free. Premiums paid by you personally: No tax relief, but payout is tax-free. Best structure: Employer pays, gross payout makes up for taxable benefit.

**Q: Income protection vs critical illness cover?**  
A: Income protection: Pays monthly income if you can't work (any illness/injury). Critical illness: One-time lump sum if diagnosed with specific condition (cancer, heart attack). Most people need both.

---

### Critical Illness

**Q: What conditions are covered by critical illness insurance?**  
A: Common: Cancer, heart attack, stroke, major organ transplant, multiple sclerosis, kidney failure. Policies cover 30-50 conditions. Read definitions carefully (some cancers excluded, conditions must be severe).

**Q: How does the payout work?**  
A: Lump sum payment (e.g., €100k) on diagnosis of covered condition. Use for: mortgage payoff, treatments, income replacement during recovery. One-time payment (not recurring like income protection).

**Q: Critical illness vs life insurance?**  
A: Life insurance pays on death. Critical illness pays on diagnosis of serious illness (while alive). Both needed: life insurance protects family if you die, critical illness protects you if you survive but can't work.

**Q: Can I have both life insurance and critical illness cover?**  
A: Yes, and recommended. Options: Separate policies (more expensive but both pay out), or combined policy with accelerated benefit (critical illness claim reduces life insurance payout). Choose separate for full protection.

## Lead Scoring Model

### Scoring Criteria (0-100 points)

**Demographics (20 points):**
- Age 30-50 + spouse/partner + 2+ kids = 20 (highest need)
- Age 25-30 + spouse = 15 (growing need)
- Age 50+ + kids grown = 10 (reduced urgency)
- Single, no kids, <30 = 5 (low priority)

**Financial Capacity (30 points):**
- Income >€75k = 30 (can afford comprehensive coverage)
- Income €50-75k = 25 (good budget)
- Income €30-50k = 15 (moderate)
- Income <€30k = 5 (budget constraints)

**Coverage Gaps (30 points):**
- No life insurance + family + mortgage = 30 (CRITICAL)
- Underinsured (low coverage vs needs) = 20
- Some gaps (missing income protection or pension) = 15
- Well-covered = 5 (low urgency, maintenance mode)

**Engagement (10 points):**
- Completed quiz 100% = 10
- Completed 50-99% = 5
- Abandoned <50% = 0

**Urgency Signals (10 points):**
- Recent major life change (baby, marriage, mortgage) = 10
- Timeline: "This month" or "Next 3 months" = 8
- Timeline: "This year" = 5
- Timeline: "Just exploring" = 2

### Segmentation

**Hot Lead (80-100 points):**
- Profile: Family with kids, good income, major coverage gaps, urgent timeline
- Action: Human advisor call within 24 hours
- Example: 35yo, married, 2 kids, €60k income, no life insurance, just bought house
- Conversion rate: 40-60%

**Warm Lead (50-79 points):**
- Profile: Some gaps, moderate income, interested but not urgent
- Action: Automated email nurture sequence, chatbot follow-up, advisor call in 7 days
- Example: 28yo, single, €40k income, has basic health insurance, no pension yet
- Conversion rate: 15-25%

**Cold Lead (<50 points):**
- Profile: Well-covered or low income/urgency
- Action: Newsletter signup, content marketing, revisit in 3-6 months
- Example: 22yo student, no dependents, just exploring options
- Conversion rate: 5-10%
